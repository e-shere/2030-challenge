In summary, we propose mass production of the previously stated carbon microsphere batteries. Considering the problem of ground water pollution from the plastic and old batteries in landfills1 and the well-known fatal outcome of not containing plastic bags , carbon microspheres are the solution. They make good use of otherwise useless plastic bags and greatly extend the life of a Lithium battery. 

We are running out of fossil fuels for energy and even metals such as Lithium for battery manufacture. Carbon microspheres would reduce the rate at which we use up our supplies of lithium and decrease the demand for fossil fuels.

Another point to note is that the battery market cannot be underestimated, for example, the global battery market worth is predicted to be about $120 billion US dollars by 2019. We estimate that the cost of these batteries will be from $300–500/kwh by that time.

So, carbon microsphere batteries would be a magnificent help to many of our problems, from groundwater to Lithium supply.

1 Chemicals from leaking batteries have been known to contaminate groundwater from unlined landfills.
