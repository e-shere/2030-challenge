Natural resource protection

According to research if we continue to exploit natural resouces at the current rate they will be exhausted by 2050. The major resources that we are using are fossil fuels (oil, coal and natural gas), they are formed from the remains of ancient plant and animal life. Fossil fuel burning powers our vehicles and industries, heats and cools our buildings, and runs appliances. It also produces electricity that we use for all sorts of purposes, such as lights and computers. Even though we have started oil mining just over 50 years ago, sixteen of the world's twenty largest oil fields have already reached their peak level of production (the point at which they are producing their annual oil yield).

Plastic waste management

Plastic waste is a big enviromental problem, as it can take as much as 1000 years to decompose and our plastic comsumption keeps on growing reaching about 300 million tons in 2015. Altough biodegrable plastics have been developed they only make up 7% of the plastics produced, the rest end up in land fills where they leak pollutants into the soil and water and a large part of it is floating around in the oceans threatening the lives of marine creatures.

Relatively little plastic is recycled because there are so many different types of it produced, which all have different chemical compositions, some of the added substances can be harmful to health if broken down. Plastic waste is also contaminated by materials such as paper and ink. Separating plastics from non-plastics in the recycling process, and different types of plastic from each other is labor-intensive and so far, there has been no easy solution. The production of plastics uses fossil fuels, it is estimated that 8% of global oil production goes into this both as the raw material and to release energy, and results in plastics having an energy value higher than coal. So the plastic landfills are not only an enviromental hazard but also a huge waste of resources.
